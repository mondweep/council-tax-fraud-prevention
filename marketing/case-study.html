Problem Statement

The Challenge

UK local authorities lose an estimated Â£2.4 billion annually to council tax fraud, representing approximately 15% of total council tax revenue. Traditional manual review processes are resource-intensive, inconsistent, and struggle to differentiate between genuine errors and deliberate fraud attempts.

Key Pain Points

Revenue Loss

Systematic fraud schemes cost authorities Â£50,000-Â£500,000 annually per authority

Resource Drain

Manual case reviews consume 60-80% of compliance team capacity

Inconsistent Detection

Human reviewers achieve only 45-65% accuracy in fraud classification

Vulnerable Residents

Sophisticated schemes like "cuckooing" exploit vulnerable individuals while evading detection

False Positives

Over-aggressive manual screening creates 40-50% false positive rates, damaging resident relationships

Specific Fraud Types Identified

  1. Single Person Discount Fraud (35% of fraud cases)
  2. Student Exemption Fraud (25% of fraud cases)
  3. Empty Property Fraud (20% of fraud cases)
  4. Cuckooing & Criminal Exploitation (10% of fraud cases)
  5. Property Banding Disputes (7% of fraud cases)
  6. Council Tax Reduction Fraud (3% of fraud cases)

Conclusion

This prototype demonstrates potential approaches to council tax fraud detection for UK local authorities, exploring how AI technology might support practical fraud detection expertise. The system offers a starting point for discussion about enhancing investigation capabilities whilst protecting vulnerable residents and maintaining the highest standards of fairness and accuracy.

The prototype's modular architecture and exploratory feature set provide a foundation for further development and evaluation, helping authorities consider how technology might support their fiduciary duties in the evolving landscape of council tax administration.
